Whim ✦ Field Notes

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2, 2026

Whim is a travel-discovery app: you swipe curated places, save the ones you like, and turn them into a day plan — alone or with friends. This page explains exactly what data Whim handles and why. The short version: we collect the minimum needed to make the app work, we don't sell anything, and there are no ads or trackers.

What we collect

  • Account: your email address, a password (stored as a secure hash by our auth provider — we never see it), and the display name you choose.
  • Your activity in the app: spots you save, check-ins ("Passport" stamps), and — in group rooms — the rooms you belong to and your votes on spots, which are visible to the other members of that room.
  • Location (optional): only if you tap "Near me", and only to find the closest city. Your location is used on-device for that lookup and is never stored on our servers. You can decline the permission and use everything else.

What we don't do

  • No selling or renting your data. Ever.
  • No advertising, no ad networks, no cross-app tracking.
  • No analytics that identify you personally.
  • No reading your contacts, photos, or anything else the app doesn't need.

Where your data lives

Whim runs on Supabase (database, authentication, and serverless functions). Your session token is stored on your device in the iOS Keychain. Every database table is protected by row-level security, meaning you can only ever read or write your own data (or a room's data if you're a member of that room).

Third-party services

  • Supabase — account, database, and hosting (see above).
  • Mapbox — renders the day-plan map and geocodes place names. Map tiles are requested from Mapbox when you view a map.
  • Google Routes — provides public-transit directions between the stops of your plan. Only the coordinates of the stops are sent, never your identity.
  • Pexels — the source of spot photography.

Deleting your account

Settings → Danger zone → Delete account, right in the app. This permanently deletes your account and everything attached to it — saved spots, check-ins, room memberships, and votes — immediately and irreversibly.

Changes

If this policy changes in a way that matters, we'll note it in the app and update the date at the top of this page.
